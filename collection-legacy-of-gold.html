The Collection / Creation No. I

Legacy of Gold

The house's founding creation — an all-gold tissue weave that took its master eleven months to complete.

ReleasedFounding Creation

Origin Story

Where Varenyam began.

Before there was a house, there was this single piece — commissioned not for sale but as proof that an all-tissue, all-gold Banarasi weave could still be made to nineteenth-century standards without a single shortcut.

Master Husna Begum took on the commission as a personal challenge. Its completion, after eleven months at the loom, became the founding moment of Varenyam itself.

Design Philosophy

No colour. Only light.

Legacy of Gold uses no dyed silk at all — every visible thread is either undyed tissue silk or zari, allowing the piece to shift in appearance entirely according to the light around it: pale gold at dawn, deep amber under candlelight.

Materials & Craftsmanship

What it is made of, and by whom.

Silk

Undyed Tissue Silk

Left in its natural pale-gold state, without any dye process.

Zari

Pure Gold Zari

Used throughout the weave, not only in the border — a rare and costly choice.

Hours

~700 Hours

Woven entirely by Master Husna Begum over eleven months.
