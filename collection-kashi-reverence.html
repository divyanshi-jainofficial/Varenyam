The Collection / सृष्टि III

Kashi Reverence

An offering to the city of its origin, carrying a temple-spire motif drawn from the ghats themselves.

ReservedHand-woven, Single Edition

Origin Story

Drawn from the skyline of Kashi.

Master Vinod Kumar Sahu spent three mornings sketching the silhouette of temple spires along the Dashashwamedh Ghat before settling on the proportions used in this creation's border — a rhythm of spires repeated thirty-one times across the length of the pallu.

Each spire is woven, not printed, with a deliberate variation in height intended to echo the irregular skyline of the ghats rather than a uniform, mechanical pattern.

Inspiration

A city woven into cloth.

Banaras is rarely permitted to be the subject of a Banarasi sari — usually it is only ever the place of manufacture. Kashi Reverence inverts this, making the city itself the motif, rendered in deep maroon silk against a border of antique gold zari.

Materials & Craftsmanship

What it is made of, and by whom.

Silk

Deep Maroon Mulberry

Dyed in small batches to preserve depth of colour across the full length.

Zari

Antique Gold Zari

Oxidised for a muted, aged finish rather than a bright modern sheen.

Hours

~310 Hours

Woven solely by Master Vinod Kumar Sahu over six months.

Edition

One of One

The skyline motif is retired from future production.
