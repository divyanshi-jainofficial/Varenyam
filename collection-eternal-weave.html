The Collection / सृष्टि VII

The Eternal Weave

A reconstruction of a nineteenth-century Kashi motif, thought lost to most living archives.

Privately AvailableHand-woven, Single Edition

Origin Story

Found in a half-burnt ledger.

In 2019, a fragment of a weaving ledger surviving a flood in a Madanpura workshop revealed a motif notation no longer in active use — a meandering vine pattern interspersed with a temple-bell border, dated to the 1880s.

Master Rajendra Prasad, whose family had once woven from the same workshop, recognised the notation immediately. The Eternal Weave is his reconstruction of that pattern, rendered in full for the first time in over a century.

Design Philosophy

Continuity over invention.

Rather than reinterpret the motif for contemporary taste, Master Prasad chose to preserve its original proportions exactly — including a slight asymmetry in the temple-bell border believed to be intentional, a quiet signature of the original anonymous weaver.

The result is a piece that reads less as a modern creation than as a recovered artifact.
